01 Philosophy
The Belief System
People teams should be deeply connected to the business rather than operate as a separate function. Culture isn't about perks or events — it's about the behaviors we reward, the standards we uphold, and the environment we create for people to do meaningful work.

02
What Most HR Teams Get Wrong
01
Activities over problems
Engagement surveys, town halls, and policies matter — but only when they're solving a real, named problem. Too often they become ends in themselves, disconnected from what people actually need.
02
Initiatives over trust
People rarely leave because there weren't enough programs. They leave when there's a lack of trust, growth, feedback, or clarity. No initiative can fix a broken feedback culture.
03
Happiness over clarity
Culture is not about making employees happy — it's about creating clarity, trust, growth, and alignment toward a common goal. Happiness follows; it isn't the target.
04
HR as a separate function
The best HR work happens when you understand the business deeply — its revenue drivers, its bottlenecks, the language of its leaders. Without that, you're just a support function.

03 A Problem I'm Proud of Solving
Recognition was happening — but inconsistently. People were putting in real effort with no shared signal for what good looked like.

At Zocket, rapid growth meant the company was adding people faster than culture could catch up. Recognition existed, but it was ad hoc — which meant it reinforced nothing in particular.

Rather than introducing awards for the sake of it, I worked with leaders to build a structured Rewards & Recognition framework where monthly achievements fed into quarterly recognition at town halls. The goal wasn't just to reward people — it was to create shared clarity around what good performance looked like, and celebrate it publicly.

What I was happiest about wasn't the framework itself. It was seeing people genuinely appreciate being recognised by their peers and leaders — and leaders becoming invested in celebrating their teams.

What It Created
01
A shared language for what "good" looked like across teams
02
Peer-led recognition that felt genuine, not performative
03
Leaders invested in publicly celebrating their teams
04
80%+ positive sentiment in pulse check-ins
People don't stay because of compensation or perks. They stay when they feel valued, seen, and connected to something bigger than themselves.
